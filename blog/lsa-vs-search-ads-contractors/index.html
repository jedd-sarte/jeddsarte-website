Google LSA vs. Search Ads for Contractors

A practical breakdown of how Google Local Services Ads and Google Search Ads work together for contractors - and when to prioritize one over the other based on your business situation.

If you're a contractor running Google advertising - or thinking about starting - you have two distinct options: Google Local Services Ads (LSA) and Google Search Ads. They appear in different places, charge differently, and attract different types of leads. Understanding which to prioritize can save significant budget and produce better booked jobs faster.

What Google Local Services Ads Actually Are

Local Services Ads appear at the very top of Google search results - above both regular Search Ads and organic listings. They show a small card with your business name, star rating, years in business, and a phone number or message button. You pay per lead, not per click, and only for leads that meet specific criteria (right service type, right location).

To run LSA, you go through Google's verification process: background check, license verification, and insurance verification. Once verified, you receive a "Google Guaranteed" badge that appears on your ad. This badge is a meaningful trust signal - according to Google's LSA documentation, the Google Guarantee means Google will reimburse customers up to $2,000 if they're unsatisfied with the work, subject to terms.

What Google Search Ads Do

Standard Google Search Ads appear below the LSA section in text ad format. You pay per click rather than per lead, which means you pay for every visitor to your website regardless of whether they contact you. You have full control over ad copy, keywords, landing pages, and bid strategies. There is no verification requirement beyond a standard Google Ads account.

Search Ads can target a much broader range of queries than LSA - including research-phase keywords, service-specific long-tail terms, and competitor-adjacent searches. You can run advanced bidding strategies, retargeting, and audience layering that LSA does not support.

Key Differences That Actually Matter

  • Payment model: LSA charges per lead, Search charges per click. In practice, LSA tends to have lower cost per actual lead for straightforward service categories
  • Control: Search Ads offer full creative and targeting control. LSA gives you almost none - Google determines when and how your ad shows
  • Lead quality: LSA leads tend to be higher intent (users calling directly from the ad) but lower volume. Search can drive more traffic with higher variance in quality
  • Setup speed: LSA takes 1-4 weeks to get verified and approved. Search Ads can go live in 24-48 hours
  • Geographic targeting: Both offer local targeting, but Search Ads give you more precise control over radius, zip codes, and exclusion zones

The dispute credit advantage: LSA includes a dispute mechanism - if you receive a lead that doesn't match your service type or location, you can dispute it for a credit. This makes the pay-per-lead model less risky than it sounds. Use this aggressively for out-of-area calls and irrelevant service requests - it meaningfully lowers your effective cost per qualified lead.

Which to Run First

The answer depends on your current situation:

  1. New business with no review base: Start with Search Ads. LSA's ranking algorithm is heavily influenced by your review count and rating. With zero reviews, you will appear near the bottom of LSA results and pay for low-quality position. Build reviews through existing customers first.
  2. Established business with 20+ reviews and 4.5+ rating: Start with LSA. The Google Guaranteed badge and review social proof combined with the top-of-page position will produce the fastest cost-efficient leads.
  3. Seasonal surge (HVAC in summer, roofing after storm season): Use both together during peak months. The combined above-fold presence compounds visibility dramatically.
  4. Service category with high search volume and specific query types: Prioritize Search Ads for the targeting control. LSA works best for generic service queries; Search Ads are better for specific service variants and geographic modifiers.

Running Both Together: The Right Structure

For most established contractors, the optimal setup is LSA and Search Ads running simultaneously with distinct roles:

  • LSA handles the high-intent "hire now" queries that convert directly from the search results page
  • Search Ads capture research-phase and specific service queries that LSA cannot target
  • Search Ads run retargeting to re-engage visitors who clicked your LSA but didn't call immediately
  • Budget split: start 60% LSA / 40% Search, adjust based on cost-per-booked-job from each source

Track both channels at the booked job level, not just the lead level. Some contractors find LSA leads close at a higher rate even if the raw cost per lead is similar to Search.
